# Freeze and Float Strategy

## When should I use float?

Use float when you accept your current allotment but still want a chance at a higher preference in later rounds, and you understand you must complete round formalities on time. Float keeps you in the upgradation process while holding the current seat provisionally.

## When should I freeze?

Freeze when you are satisfied with your allotted seat and do not want further upgradation attempts. Freezing is appropriate when further rounds are unlikely to improve your outcome meaningfully or when the risk of losing a good seat outweighs possible gains.

## Which JoSAA option is safer — float or freeze?

Freeze is safer if you want certainty and your current seat is already a strong fit. Float carries strategic upside if better preferences might open, but requires careful tracking of deadlines and reporting rules. Safety depends on your rank range and remaining preferences, not a universal rule.

## Should I float if I got a lower preference branch?

Floating can make sense if higher preferences might still be reachable in upcoming rounds based on official trends — without treating past data as a guarantee. If the gap is large and preferences above are unlikely, freezing may reduce stress.

## When should I use slide instead of float?

Slide (branch sliding) applies when you want to remain at the same institute but seek a better branch. Use it when institute matters more than branch change across colleges. Follow official JoSAA definitions for the current year.

## Should I use slide?

Use slide when you accept your current institute but want a better branch there in later rounds. It is not for changing institutes. Compare slide with float based on whether you want to keep the same college while seeking branch upgradation.

## When to float in JoSAA counselling?

Float when your allotted seat is acceptable for now but you still want a shot at a higher preference in upcoming rounds. Confirm you can meet reporting deadlines and understand that float keeps you in the upgradation pool.

## When to freeze in JoSAA counselling?

Freeze when you are content with the allotted seat and do not want to risk losing it for uncertain upgrades. This is often wise when further improvement is unlikely for your rank band.
