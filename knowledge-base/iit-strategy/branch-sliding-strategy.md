# Branch Sliding Strategy

## Is branch sliding useful?

Branch sliding can be useful when you are happy with your institute but want a higher-preference branch there. It is less useful if your main goal is to change institutes. Evaluate whether remaining branches at the same college are meaningfully better for you.

## When should I slide vs float?

Float can change both institute and branch if a higher overall preference is allotted. Slide focuses on branch improvement within the same institute. Choose slide when the institute is right but branch is not; choose float when a better institute-branch combination is still possible.

## Should I slide if I already have a decent branch?

Sliding only makes sense if higher branches on your list at that institute are significantly better for your goals. If current branch already matches your plan, freezing may be simpler than chasing small upgrades.

## What are risks of relying on branch sliding?

You may not get the upgraded branch if seats do not open in your category and quota. Always maintain a realistic choice list and meet reporting deadlines. Do not assume sliding will automatically improve outcomes.

## How do I order choices to support sliding strategy?

Group strong institute-branch combinations together and place preferred branches at the same institute above weaker branches there. Official choice ordering rules should be followed as published on the JoSAA portal each year.
