# Common Counselling Mistakes

## What are common JoSAA counselling mistakes?

Common mistakes include: filling choices based on hearsay cutoffs, freezing too early out of fear, ignoring quota and category rules, not reading reporting deadlines, and chasing only brand without branch fit. Use official JoSAA data and counsellor guidance.

## Is freezing too early a mistake?

It can be if you might still get significantly better preferences in later rounds and your rank historically moves into those seats. It is not a mistake if your current seat is already a top realistic choice and further waiting adds stress without likely benefit.

## Is chasing college brand over branch a mistake?

It often becomes a mistake when students dislike the branch they accepted and struggle academically or switch paths later. Brand helps, but four years of the wrong field is costly. Align brand with genuine interest.

## Is ignoring home state and other state quota a mistake?

Yes. Quota rules affect which seats you compete for at NITs and other institutes. Candidates who ignore quota may misunderstand why certain options are or are not reachable. Check official quota definitions in the brochure.

## Should I rely on social media cutoff posts?

Relying on unverified social media cutoffs is risky. Use official JoSAA opening and closing ranks from the portal. Informal numbers may be wrong, outdated, or for a different category or quota.
