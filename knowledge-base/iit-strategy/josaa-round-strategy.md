# JoSAA Round Strategy

## Should I wait for later JoSAA rounds?

Waiting can help if your rank may reach higher preferences in later rounds based on historical movement patterns — but past movement is not a guarantee. Consider how close your rank is to official published data and whether you can accept interim seats with float.

## How should I order my JoSAA choice list?

Place choices in true preference order: institute-branch combinations you would actually accept, from most to least desired. Avoid putting unrealistic dream options far above safe accepts without understanding round dynamics.

## Is early round acceptance a mistake?

Accepting in an early round is not automatically wrong if the seat is already among your top realistic preferences. Waiting for perfect outcomes can backfire if preferences do not materialize. Balance ambition with acceptable backups.

## What is a safe vs ambitious choice list strategy?

Include a mix: a few ambitious choices, several realistic targets, and safe accepts you can live with. Safe does not mean lowest only — it means options aligned with your rank band using official data, not rumors.

## When should I consider CSAB in my strategy?

Consider CSAB if you still have realistic preferences among NITs, IIITs, or CFTIs after JoSAA regular rounds and vacant seats match your goals. CSAB is not a substitute for careful JoSAA choice filling. Read the official CSAB brochure each year.
