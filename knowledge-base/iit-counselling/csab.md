# CSAB Counselling

## What is CSAB?

CSAB (Central Seat Allocation Board) conducts special rounds of counselling for vacant seats in NITs, IIITs, and CFTIs after JoSAA regular rounds end. It gives candidates another opportunity to secure seats that remain unfilled.

## How is CSAB different from JoSAA?

JoSAA handles the main multi-round allocation for IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs. CSAB focuses on special rounds for leftover seats, mainly in NITs, IIITs, and CFTIs. IIT seats are generally not part of CSAB special rounds.

## Who can participate in CSAB?

Eligibility for CSAB is defined in the official CSAB information brochure each year. Typically, candidates who were registered in JoSAA and meet the special-round criteria can participate, subject to seat availability and category rules.

## What is a CSAB special round?

A CSAB special round is an additional counselling round where vacant seats are offered to eligible registered candidates. Choice filling and seat acceptance rules are published in the CSAB portal for that academic year.

## When does CSAB counselling happen?

CSAB counselling is conducted after JoSAA regular rounds conclude. Dates and registration windows are announced on the official CSAB website and should be verified each year from official sources.
