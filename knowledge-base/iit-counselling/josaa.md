# JoSAA Counselling

## What is JoSAA?

JoSAA (Joint Seat Allocation Authority) is the central body that conducts seat allocation for IITs, NITs, IIITs, and other GFTIs based on JEE Main and JEE Advanced ranks. It coordinates multiple rounds of online counselling where candidates fill choices, get seat allotments, and accept or modify options.

## How many rounds are there in JoSAA?

JoSAA typically conducts six regular rounds of seat allocation. After the regular rounds, CSAB may conduct additional special rounds for remaining vacant seats in NITs, IIITs, and CFTIs. The exact schedule is published on the official JoSAA website each year.

## What happens in each JoSAA round?

In each round, candidates who have registered and paid fees can submit or reorder their college-branch choices. The system allocates seats based on rank, category, quota, and choice order. Candidates must then log in to accept, float, slide, or freeze the allotted seat before the round deadline.

## Who is eligible for JoSAA counselling?

Candidates who qualify in JEE Main and/or JEE Advanced (for IITs) and meet the eligibility criteria published for that year can register for JoSAA. Eligibility includes age limits, subject requirements, and qualifying exam criteria as per official notices.

## What is JoSAA registration?

JoSAA registration is the mandatory online process where candidates create a counselling account, pay the registration fee, and become eligible to fill choices in the seat allocation rounds. Without registration, a candidate cannot participate in JoSAA counselling.
