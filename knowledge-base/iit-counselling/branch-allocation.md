# Branch Allocation

## How does branch allocation work in JoSAA?

Branch allocation depends on your rank, category, quota, seat availability, and the order of choices you submit. Higher preference choices are considered first when your rank is eligible for a seat in a given round.

## Can I change my branch after allotment?

During active JoSAA rounds, you may use float or slide options to seek a better branch or institute if rules allow. After accepting a seat and completing admission formalities, branch changes follow institute rules and are generally not guaranteed through counselling.

## What is choice filling order?

Choice filling order is the priority list of institute-branch combinations you submit. The system tries to give you the highest choice on your list for which your rank and category are eligible in that round.

## What is a dual degree or special program seat?

Some IITs offer dual degree or special program seats listed separately in the JoSAA choice list. These have distinct seat codes and eligibility; candidates should read the official seat matrix before filling choices.

## Which factors affect branch allotment?

Key factors include JEE rank (CRL or category rank as applicable), category, home/other state quota for NITs, seat availability, and your choice order. Personal preferences should be aligned with realistic rank ranges from official data.
