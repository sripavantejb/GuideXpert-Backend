# Rank Types

## What is CRL rank?

CRL (Common Rank List) rank is your overall rank among all candidates in JEE Main or JEE Advanced, without category reservation. It is used for general category seat allocation and as a reference merit position.

## What is OBC-NCL rank?

OBC-NCL rank is the rank of a candidate within the OBC-NCL category list. Seat allocation for OBC-NCL reserved seats uses this category rank, not the CRL rank, subject to eligibility certificate requirements.

## What is category rank?

Category rank is the merit position within a specific reserved category such as OBC-NCL, SC, ST, or GEN-EWS. JoSAA uses the appropriate rank type for seat allocation in each category quota.

## What is JEE Advanced rank?

JEE Advanced rank is the merit rank assigned to candidates who appear in JEE Advanced. IIT seat allocation is based on JEE Advanced ranks and category ranks as per JoSAA rules for that year.

## What is the difference between CRL and category rank?

CRL rank compares you with all candidates nationally. Category rank compares you only within your reserved category. Reserved seats are allotted using category rank; open/general seats use CRL or category rules as published.
