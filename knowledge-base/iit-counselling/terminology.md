# Counselling Terminology

## What is choice locking?

Choice locking is the deadline by which candidates must finalize their preference list for an upcoming allocation round. After locking, choices may not be edited until the next choice-filling window opens.

## What is a mock allotment?

Mock allotment is a practice result published by JoSAA before a real round to help candidates understand likely outcomes based on current choices and ranks. It is indicative only and not the final allotment.

## What is a GFTI?

GFTI (Government Funded Technical Institute) refers to participating technical institutes in JoSAA other than IITs, NITs, and IIITs. They have separate seat codes in the choice list and follow JoSAA allocation rules.

## What is a spot round?

Spot round or special round refers to additional counselling rounds (often under CSAB) to fill vacant seats after regular JoSAA rounds. Rules and participating institutes are announced officially each year.

## What is document verification in counselling?

Document verification confirms category certificates, identity, and academic eligibility during admission. Candidates must carry original documents as per institute and JoSAA checklists when reporting for admission.
