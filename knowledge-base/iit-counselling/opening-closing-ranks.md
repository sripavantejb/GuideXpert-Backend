# Opening and Closing Ranks

## What are opening and closing ranks?

Opening rank is the best (lowest) rank that received a seat in a particular institute-branch-quota combination in a given round. Closing rank is the worst (highest) rank that still received a seat. They describe historical seat allocation ranges, not personal predictions.

## Where can I find opening and closing ranks?

JoSAA publishes round-wise seat allotment statistics on the official portal after each round. These should be checked from official JoSAA data for the relevant year and round.

## Do opening and closing ranks change every round?

Yes. As candidates accept, float, slide, or freeze seats, vacancy and competition change, so opening and closing ranks can shift between rounds and between years.

## Can I use last year's closing rank to plan choices?

Last year's closing ranks are useful for rough planning only. They are not guarantees for the current year because seat availability, number of applicants, and ranks change annually.

## What is a seat matrix?

The seat matrix is the official table listing available seats by institute, branch, category, and quota for JoSAA counselling. It is published on the JoSAA website and should be used together with historical allotment data for choice planning.
