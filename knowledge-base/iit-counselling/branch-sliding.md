# Branch Sliding and Floating

## What is branch sliding?

In JoSAA, slide (branch sliding) means you accept your currently allotted seat but remain open to moving to a higher preferred choice in later rounds if your rank becomes eligible. You keep your current seat unless a better choice is allotted.

## What is freezing and floating?

Freeze means you accept the allotted seat and stop participating in further upgradation. Float means you accept the seat for now but stay in counselling for a possible upgrade to a higher choice in later rounds. Freeze is final for that admission path once confirmed as per rules.

## What is float?

Float is a JoSAA option where you provisionally accept your allotted seat while staying eligible for upgradation to a higher preference in subsequent rounds. If no upgrade happens, you retain the current seat.

## What is slide?

Slide is similar to float in that you accept the current allotment but remain in the upgradation process. Official JoSAA documentation uses freeze, float, and slide with specific definitions candidates must follow on the portal each year.

## When should I freeze my seat?

Freeze when you are satisfied with your allotted institute-branch and do not want further upgradation risk. Candidates close to the last rounds often freeze to secure admission. The decision should be based on realistic upgrade chances from official round data, not assumptions.
