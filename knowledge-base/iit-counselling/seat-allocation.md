# Seat Allocation

## How does JoSAA seat allocation work?

JoSAA uses a computerized seat allocation process based on candidate ranks, categories, quotas, seat availability, and choice order. Allotment results are published online after each round for candidates to accept or modify options.

## What is seat acceptance?

Seat acceptance is the step where a candidate confirms their allotted seat by choosing freeze, float, or slide within the deadline. Failure to complete this step in time can lead to forfeiture of the allotment for that round.

## What is reporting to an institute?

After seat acceptance and payment of fees as required, candidates must complete reporting or admission formalities at the allotted institute within published timelines. Procedures vary by institute and are listed in JoSAA and institute notices.

## What happens if I reject a seat?

If you do not accept an allotment or miss deadlines, the seat may be cancelled for that round and you may participate in later rounds if still eligible. Exact consequences are defined in the JoSAA business rules for that year.

## What is seat withdrawal?

Seat withdrawal is the formal process to exit an accepted seat under allowed conditions, such as before certain deadlines or when upgrading. Rules and financial implications are specified in official JoSAA documents and should be verified each year.
